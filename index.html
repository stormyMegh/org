Family Trip to Jaisalmer, Rajasthan

By Megh K Patel

Intro Explore Adventure Culture Gallery Reflection

Diwali Vacation Begins

This Diwali, our family set out on a magical journey to the Golden City of Jaisalmer. The excitement was in the air as we packed our bags and hit the road. The drive through Rajasthan was filled with scenic beauty, rustic charm, and endless stretches of desert.

We arrived at a beautiful resort just before sunset. The first thing we did? Jumped into the swimming pool to cool off and relax after the long journey.

Exploring the Golden City

Our exploration began with the majestic Jaisalmer Fort, a golden sandstone marvel that seemed to rise from the desert itself. Inside, we wandered through narrow alleys, visited ancient temples, and shopped for local handicrafts.

We also visited the famous Patwon Ki Haveli, where the intricate carvings and historical stories transported us to another era.

Adventure in the Desert

The next day was packed with adventure. We went boating at the serene Gadisar Lake, surrounded by temples and ghats. Then came the highlight—paragliding over the golden dunes! The view from above was breathtaking.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, we headed to our desert camp. We spent the night camping under the stars, sharing stories around a bonfire and enjoying the peaceful desert night.

Cultural Delights

One of the most memorable parts of our trip was attending a cultural music event. Folk musicians played traditional instruments while dancers performed in colorful attire. The rhythm, the colors, and the energy made us feel truly immersed in Rajasthani culture.

Exploring Jaisalmer Fort

We wandered through the golden walls of Jaisalmer Fort, discovering ancient temples and vibrant markets.

Dune Driving

Thrilling sensory experience, with the wind whipping through my hair, open landscape stretched out endlessly before me.

Paragliding & Boating

Thrill met serenity as we soared above the dunes and glided across Gadisar Lake in a peaceful boat ride.

Fun Fact

Jaisalmer is called the "Golden City" because of the yellow sandstone used in its architecture, which glows at sunset.

Final Thoughts

This trip was more than just a vacation—it was a journey full of discovery, bonding, and joy. From thrilling adventures to peaceful moments under the stars, Jaisalmer gave us memories we’ll cherish forever.
